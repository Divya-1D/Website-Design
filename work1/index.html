With your goal and initial ideas in mind, it’s time to research your target audience and competitors.

What you want to find out is:

What type of information your potential customers are interested in What types of content your competitors have been publishing to capture them The best way to do it (although slightly time-consuming) is to manually go through competitors’ pages, social media groups, Q&A sites, and forums – and note down all relevant information like the topics addressed, questions asked, and types of content that you see. To give you an idea of what this kind of research could lead to, imagine you’re a personal trainer. By checking out other personal trainers’ websites, you’ve noticed that all of them have these kinds of pages and content on their site: A services page where they describe their services and pricing plans
